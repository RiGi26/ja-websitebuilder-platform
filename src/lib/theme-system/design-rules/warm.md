# Design Rules ‚Äî mood: `warm`

> Anchored to **16 production themes** currently tagged `mood: 'warm'`:
> `kuliner-rustic`, `kerajinan-tenun`, `rumah-natural`, `herbal-jamu`, `warung-rakyat`, `finedining-hearth`, `estetik-rosegold`, `wellness-terra`, `reguler-ceria`, `islami-emas`, `profesional-warm`, `korporat-netral`, `wisata-senja`, `akomodasi-kayu`, `jurnal-hangat`, `lokal-hangat`

## 1. Mission
**Warm = homemade & menggugah selera.** This mood says "made by hand, made for you" ‚Äî
the opposite of a chain-store sterility. It belongs to brands whose edge IS the human
touch: warung, heritage F&B, herbal/jamu, natural-living goods, hospitality. The
customer should feel *invited in*, not marketed at. Cream/terracotta/amber tones,
serif-leaning headings, generous roundness ‚Äî every token earns its place by making the
page feel like it was set by a person, not generated by a template.

**Rules (anchored to the table above):**
- Primary **must** read ‚â• 4.5:1 against `page` for any *normal-weight body-size* use.
  For large/bold CTA text on `primary` (the typical `onPrimary` case), the practical
  floor proven in production is **~3.3:1** (see `herbal-jamu`, `wellness-terra`,
  `reguler-ceria` ‚Äî all pass WCAG's *large-text* 3:1 bar). New themes **should** target
  ‚â• 4:1 for `onPrimary`/`primary` and **must not** ship below 3:1.
- `primary` **must not** be used for body copy ‚Äî it's an accent, not an ink. Use `ink`/`muted`.
- Page **must** stay in the cream/ivory family (`#FBF6EE`‚Äì`#F8F4EC` range, all production
  examples sit there) ‚Äî pure white (`#FFFFFF`) reads "clinical", kills the homemade feel.
- Display/body **should** pair serif-display + sans-body (8 of 16 production themes do ‚Äî
  the dominant pattern) for an "artisan label" feel; sans+sans (3 of 16, `sama` rows)
  is acceptable for a more contemporary-warm lean but **must not** use two serifs ‚Äî that
  tips into `luxury`, diluting the mood's identity.
- Tracking **should** stay in the ‚àí0.005em to ‚àí0.01em band (tight-but-readable); avoid
  the aggressive ‚àí0.02em+ negative tracking that belongs to `bold`.

## 3. Layout guidance (derived ‚Äî which archetypes this mood actually uses)

- **Hero:** `centered` √ó11, `split` √ó5
- **Features:** `grid` √ó15, `rows` √ó1
- **Padding:** `airy` √ó9, `normal` √ó7
- **Alignment:** `center` √ó11, `left` √ó5

- **Hero `centered` is the default lean** (11/16) ‚Äî it puts the product/place at the
  literal center of attention, mirroring how a host presents a dish. **Should** be the
  first choice for F&B/hospitality/herbal. `split` (5/16) is acceptable for brands with
  a stronger editorial angle (`profesional-warm`, `korporat-netral`) but **must not**
  become the default ‚Äî it reads more "agency" than "homemade", and dilutes the mood.
- **Features `grid`** dominates (15/16) ‚Äî cards read as a menu/catalog, which fits
  warm's "browse and pick" posture. `rows` (numbered list) is the rare exception for
  narrative-led brands; **should not** be picked just for variety (VARIASI #6 governs
  *across* gaya, not by forcing odd choices *within* one mood).
- **`pad: airy`** edges out `normal` (9 vs 7) ‚Äî warm benefits from breathing room around
  photography (food/craft/place shots need space to read as "curated", not "cramped").
  New themes **should** lean airy when the hero/showcase carries photography.
- **`align: center`** dominates (11/16), matching the centered-hero lean ‚Äî coherent
  vertical rhythm. `left` pairs with `split` hero; **must** keep hero/align consistent
  (don't mix `centered` hero with `left` align ‚Äî it reads unbalanced).

## 4. States
- **Default:** `surface` cards on `page` background, `shadow.sm` ‚Äî quiet, not flat
  (warm **must not** ship shadowless cards; that reads cold/corporate).
- **Hover (cards/images):** **must** step the shadow up one tier (`sm`‚Üí`md`) and/or a
  subtle scale (~1.01‚Äì1.02); **must not** rely on opacity-only changes ‚Äî opacity shifts
  read as "disabled", the opposite of inviting.
- **Hover (CTA/buttons):** **should** warm the `primary` by a touch (mix toward
  `heroTo`) rather than darken-to-black ‚Äî darkening reads aggressive, off-mood.
- **Focus-visible:** **must** show a visible ring in `primary` at ‚â• 2px against both
  `surface` and `page` ‚Äî warm's high `ink`/`page` contrast (11.95‚Äì16.84:1, see ¬ß2)
  makes this cheap to get right; don't skip it.
- **Active/pressed:** **should** compress shadow back toward `sm` (gives tactile
  "pressed" feedback) ‚Äî pairs with `/make-interfaces-feel-better` micro-interaction guidance.
- **Disabled:** **must** drop to `muted` text on `border`-tinted surface, never grayscale
  the whole palette ‚Äî full desaturation breaks warm's identity even in disabled state.
- **Loading/empty:** **should** keep the cream `page`/`surface` family ‚Äî a stark white
  skeleton screen breaks immersion harder in warm than in any other mood (the cream/ivory
  floor IS the brand cue, see ¬ß2 page-color rule).

## 5. Accessibility (promote scorecard bars to upfront guidance)
- [ ] Text/bg contrast **must** be ‚â• 4.5:1 (production floor for this mood: see ¬ß2 above)
- [ ] Focus rings **must** be visible against `surface` and `primary`
- [ ] Touch targets (CTAs) **must** be ‚â• 44px
- [ ] Motion **must** respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- **Mood-specific:** warm is *all light-page* in production (no dark variant exists or
  is needed ‚Äî that would be `luxury`'s territory). Watch `muted` text on `surface`:
  the cream-on-cream combinations can drift below 4.5:1 faster than dark-mode pairings
  do ‚Äî always check `muted`/`surface`, not just `ink`/`page`, when defining a new theme.
- **`onPrimary`/`primary` is the known soft spot** (¬ß2: production floor ~3.3:1, two
  themes ‚Äî `herbal-jamu` 3.34, `kuliner-rustic` family ~2.9 in sibling moods ‚Äî sit close
  to the large-text-only 3:1 line). **Must** verify CTA text is large/bold enough to
  qualify for the WCAG large-text exception, or lighten `onPrimary`/darken `primary`.
